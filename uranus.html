Uranus

Age 4.567 billion years

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third largest planet in the Solar System. It is named after the Greek god of the sky and is known for its bright blue color. Uranus has 27 known moons and 13 rings made of ice, rock and dust. Uranus' atmosphere is composed primarily of hydrogen, helium and methane, which gives it its bright blue color. The average temperature on Uranus is -224 degrees Celsius. Uranus has powerful magnetic fields and radiation belts that create strong magnetic storms and radiation. Research on Uranus began in the 20th century and continues to this day. In the 21st century, space missions have been sent to study the planet, its moons and rings, including Voyagers, Cassini and New Horizons.
