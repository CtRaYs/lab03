Neptune
Weight 1.024×1026 Kg
Radius 24622 km
Age 4.5 billion years
Min. temperature -218 °C
Max. temperature -203 °C
Neptune is the eighth and last planet of our solar system. It is named after the Roman god of the sea and is known for its deep blue tones and ring system. Neptune has 14 known moons and an atmosphere composed primarily of hydrogen, helium and methane. The average temperature on Neptune is -218 degrees Celsius. Neptune has a powerful magnetic field and radiation belts that create strong magnetic storms and radiation. Research on Neptune began in the 20th century and continued into the 21st century. In 2011, the Voyager 2 space mission flew past Neptune and transmitted information about the planet. Currently, the study of Neptune continues using telescopes and space missions.
